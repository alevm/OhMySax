Placeholder photos — real horns coming

The photos on the Collection page are reference images, not my actual instruments. They're there to give you a visual sense of each model while I work through the queue and shoot proper photos of each horn on the bench. Some are the exact model; others are the closest cousin I could find under a free license.

All images below are from Wikimedia Commons under open licenses. Attribution follows the terms of each license.

  • Yanagisawa A-992 — reference image is a Yanagisawa A-9932J (closest visual match on Commons). By Nabokov, Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA 3.0.
  • Yanagisawa A-50 / A-500 — no specific image available. Generic alto saxophone fallback. By Rama, Wikimedia Commons (Paris, Musée de la Musique E.1685), CC BY-SA 3.0 FR.
  • Yamaha YAS-21 — reference image is a Yamaha YAS-25 (same family, no free YAS-21 image found). Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA 3.0.
  • Martin Handcraft — gold Martin alto. By The United States Army Band (via Flickr), Wikimedia Commons, Public Domain.
  • Conn New Wonder Series I — reference image is a 1927 Conn New Wonder Series 2 (close cousin). By Nabokov, Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA 4.0.
  • Buescher 400 — reference image is a 1915 Buescher Truetone (no free Buescher 400 available; this is an earlier Buescher alto). By Nabokov, Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA 4.0.
  • Selmer Bundy — Selmer Bundy II alto. By Jana C. (User:Staticjana), Wikimedia Commons, Public Domain.
  • Couesnon Monopole, Ida Maria Grassi, Sequoia, Gear4Music — no specific images available for these models. All use the same generic alto fallback. By Rama, Wikimedia Commons (Paris, Musée de la Musique E.1685), CC BY-SA 3.0 FR.
